My favorite species (ranked)

There are 18 recognized penguin species. These are my top four. I could defend it.

  1. Emperor Penguin (Aptenodytes forsteri)
    • Only bird that breeds in Antarctic winter.
    • Males fast for ~115 days while incubating the egg.
    • Dive depths routinely exceed 500 meters.
  2. Adélie Penguin (Pygoscelis adeliae)
    • The classic cartoon penguin silhouette.
    • Massive colonies — Cape Crozier has ~270,000 breeding pairs.
    • Steal pebbles from each other's nests like tiny criminals.
  3. Gentoo Penguin (Pygoscelis papua)
    • Fastest swimmer of any penguin — up to 36 km/h.
    • Bright orange bill that does not look real.
    • Populations are actually growing, which is rare good news.
  4. Galápagos Penguin (Spheniscus mendiculus)
    • Only penguin species found north of the equator.
    • The rarest penguin — fewer than 2,000 mature individuals left.
    • Proof that penguins don't need ice to exist.

Facts I had AI make

  • A group of penguins on land is called a waddle. In the water, it's a raft. This is correct and beautiful.
  • Penguins don't have teeth. They have backward-facing spines on their tongues instead. Horrifying. Effective.
  • The fossil record includes a species called Anthropornis that was the size of an adult human.
  • Penguins drink saltwater and excrete the salt through a gland above their eyes.
  • They recognize their mate's call out of thousands of others in a colony.
